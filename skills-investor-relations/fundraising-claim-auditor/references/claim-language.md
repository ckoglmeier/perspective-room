# Claim Language

## Language Ladder

- `proves`: rarely use; requires direct evidence, meaningful baseline, and clear scope.
- `shows`: direct evidence in the stated scope.
- `suggests`: early, narrow, or noisy evidence.
- `points toward`: useful directional signal for the next test.
- `does not yet show`: claim is tempting but unsupported.

## Common Rewrites

- "We have product-market fit" -> "Early usage suggests a painful workflow and a repeatable wedge; retention and expansion are still the next proof."
- "Our model is safer" -> "In this benchmark, the system reduced unsafe actions from X to Y under the tested workflow conditions."
- "Customers are demanding this" -> "Named customer conversations and pilots show demand in this segment; repeatable conversion is still being tested."
- "We own the category" -> "We are defining a focused wedge in [category] where current tools leave [gap]."
